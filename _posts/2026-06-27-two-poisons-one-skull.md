Everybody in the smoking circle has a verdict and almost nobody can define the damage. Weed's harmless. Booze is poison. Or flip it, depending on whose dad drank. So before the comment war, before somebody says *it's a plant, bro*, let us do the unglamorous part and measure the wreckage — and then, because the honest blog owes it to you, count the one column where the plant actually earns the white coat.

## 0x01 · the thing itself

Two substances. Two completely different ways to be bad for you. That is the whole problem with the bar argument, you are watching people compare a sprain to a stab wound and insist one of them doesn't count.

THC walks up to your CB1 receptors and sits down. Those receptors are packed into the exact rooms you'd least want a stranger in: the hippocampus, the amygdala, the cortex. Memory, fear, the thinking part. Ethanol doesn't bother with a key. It's a solvent. It just floods the whole house and depresses everything at once, and at a high enough tide, the part that runs your lungs.

Same skull. One picks a lock. The other drowns the building. Hold that image, the headlines will try to flatten both into "drugs."

## 0x02 · tonight (the short term)

**Weed, tonight.** THC jams encoding in the hippocampus, so new memory stops writing to disk. You lose the last ten minutes, the thread of the sentence, the reason you stood up. There's a nastier wrinkle the stoner mythology skips: intoxication makes you more willing to accept **false memories**, things that never happened, filed as if they did. So it isn't only that you forget what was real. Your grip on *what counted as real* gets slippery. Add slow reaction time, warped clock, and for some people a free anxiety attack. Rarely lethal. Keep that word, *rarely*, it's the whole ballgame in section eight.

**Booze, tonight.** Same memory sabotage, louder. Push it far enough and the hippocampus stops recording entirely, the **blackout**, hours of you awake and functioning and unrecorded, a ghost piloting your body. But here's the line cannabis doesn't cross: drink fast enough and ethanol turns the breathing down to zero. Acute poisoning. A dose ceiling that ends with a coroner. Weed does not have that number. Alcohol does, and people find it every weekend.

## 0x03 · the years (the long term)

**Weed, the long game.** This is where the panic posts lied to you, and where the stoners get to feel smug for exactly one paragraph. For **adults**, most of the cognitive damage appears to *lift* when you stop. Heavy users test worse on verbal memory and attention while using and in the days after, then a decent meta-analysis (Schreiner & Dunn) found the residual deficit gone after roughly a month clean. The receptors heal too, CB1 downregulates under heavy use and then climbs back toward baseline after about four weeks dry. The brain reweighs the wiring. It comes back.

**Booze, the long game.** It does not come back. Ethanol is **neurotoxic** in the literal sense, it kills cells and leaves scar. Wernicke-Korsakoff. Alcohol dementia. The cerebellum eating itself. That is not a suppressed receptor waiting to recover, that is dead tissue you do not get back. Then it works down the rest of you, liver to cirrhosis, pancreas, heart, a fistful of cancers. And the dependence is physical, the kind where **quitting cold can kill you**, withdrawal seizures, DTs, a detox that needs a hospital. Weed withdrawal is real and miserable, bad sleep, no appetite, a short temper. It does not put you in the morgue.

## 0x04 · the part that should scare you

Here's the bit both tribes skip because it ruins their slogan.

Cannabis carries one long shadow that alcohol does not lead on: the association with **psychosis and schizophrenia onset** in people already wired for it, sharpest with high-potency product and early, frequent use. Not everyone. Not a guarantee. But consistent enough that "it's just a plant" is doing a lot of unpaid labor in that sentence. Add **hyperemesis**, the long-haul heavy-user vomiting cycle nobody warns you about until you're hugging the toilet hot-showering at 4am wondering what's wrong with the one substance that was supposed to be the safe one.

And the one neither tribe wants on the flyer: the **developing brain**. Before the wiring finishes, call it twenty-five, both of these reach in during a build phase and leave fingerprints. The adult-recovery story I just sold you? It thins out hard for the teenager. The earlier the start, the less of it heals. Age of first use is the variable that quietly outranks almost everything else.

## 0x05 · the other ledger

Now the turn the harm autopsy owes you, because the same plant that smears your short-term memory also has real medicine cut into it. Not dispensary-poster medicine. FDA-letterhead medicine, the kind that survived a trial instead of a testimonial.

**CBD — Epidiolex.** Purified cannabidiol, approved, and it genuinely strangles seizures in two vicious childhood epilepsies, Dravet and Lennox-Gastaut. Kids who were seizing dozens of times a day, fewer times now. That is not a vibe. That is a label.

**THC, the synthetics.** Dronabinol and nabilone are approved for chemo nausea and for appetite when AIDS wasting is hollowing someone out. Nabiximols — Sativex, a THC:CBD spray — is prescribed across much of the world for multiple sclerosis spasticity.

Then the boring verdict nobody quotes because it doesn't fit on a flag: the 2017 National Academies review landed solid-ish evidence for **chronic pain in adults**, **MS spasticity**, and **chemo nausea**. The single most common honest reason people reach for it — pain — is also one of the few that brought receipts.

After that comes the swears-by-it tier, always thinner than the testimonials. **Sleep**, it drops you fast and then quietly robs your REM and builds tolerance, so the short win curdles over months. **Anxiety**, a literal coin flip, CBD shows a little promise while THC calms one person and hands the next a panic attack, all dose-dependent. **PTSD**, **appetite**, **inflammation** — plausible, under-proven, wildly oversold.

Two asterisks, because this is still the honest blog:

— the clean evidence is for *specific cannabinoids at a measured dose*, not a joint. Combust the flower and you drag a whole lung tab into the room. Most of "weed is medicine" is really CBD wearing weed's leather jacket.

— and the booze column on this ledger? Alcohol's one famous "benefit," the glass-of-red-saves-your-heart story, got quietly walked back. The newer reading is there's no protective dose worth chasing and the old studies were measuring sick teetotalers, not healthy abstainers. So this row is lopsided on purpose: one of these two has actual pharmacology, the other had a marketing department.

## 0x06 · the receipts (head to head)

```
                  WEED          BOOZE
acute kill         no            YES, has a number
brain cell death   ~no           yes, with scarring
recovery off it    mostly        partial at best
withdrawal         miserable     can be lethal
overall pop. harm  lower         higher (Nutt et al.)
psychosis link     yes           not the leader
real medical use   yes (some)    basically none
teen brain         harmful       harmful
```

When researchers (Nutt and the crew) ranked drug harm across every axis they could name, including harm to *other* people, alcohol topped the list. Not because the math hates fun. Because it kills acutely, scars permanently, detoxes deadly, and wrecks the people standing next to the user, not just the user.

## 0x07 · the honest caveat

Do not let "alcohol is worse" finish the sentence for you, because the sentence ends in a lie. **Worse is not safe.** By population body count and raw toxicity, yes, ethanol is the bigger monster and most experts will say so out loud. That verdict says nothing about *your* skull, *your* family history of psychosis, *your* sixteen-year-old's still-wet wiring.

And do not let the other ledger finish it either. **Medicine is not a permission slip.** Yes, it's the real thing for an epileptic kid and a chemo patient and a chronic-pain adult — at a dose, in a form, for a reason. None of that converts a teenager's daily high-THC habit into a prescription. The same molecule can be a label and a liability depending entirely on who, how much, and how young.

Both tribes are half right and that's the most annoying possible outcome. Anyone who tells you weed is harmless is wrong. Anyone who tells you it's as bad as the bottle is wrong. Anyone who tells you it's candy *or* poison missed that it's a pharmacy — stocked with both. Hold all of it. The whole truth lives in that gap, and the gap is the only honest place to stand.
